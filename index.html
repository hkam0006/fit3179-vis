World Air Pollution

This map data visualization presents the Air Quality Index (AQI) values for various countries around the world. The AQI is a standardized numerical system that assesses and communicates the quality of the air in a specific location, offering valuable insights into air pollution levels and potential health risks.

The data visualization presents a stark picture of air quality around the world, with the United States emerging as the country with the most concerning Air Quality Index (AQI) value, standing at a staggering 321.50. Following closely behind, South Africa records an AQI of 165, indicating significant air pollution challenges in the region. India, a nation grappling with air quality issues, reports an AQI of 161. These figures underscore the critical need for air quality management and environmental initiatives in these countries. The data serves as a powerful call to action, highlighting the urgency of addressing air pollution and its impact on public health and the environment on a global scale.

Deaths attributed to Air Pollution per 10,000

This graph depicts a significant and encouraging trend in air pollution-related deaths per 10,000 people from the year 1990 to 2019. Over this 29-year period, we observe a steady decrease in air pollution-related deaths globally.
